Welcome to Jaipur — city of palaces, bazaars & vibrant culture

Founded in 1727, Jaipur is Rajasthan's capital and one of India's most photogenic cities. Known as the Pink City for its terracotta-colored buildings, Jaipur blends royal history, living craft traditions, and bustling markets.

About Jaipur

An overview of the Pink City's spirit

What makes Jaipur special?

Jaipur is a planned 18th-century city, famous for its consistent pink-painted old city, majestic forts (Amber, Nahargarh), ornate palaces (Hawa Mahal, City Palace), and its role as a center for arts — gemstones, textiles, block printing and blue pottery.

How to get there

Jaipur is well-connected by road, rail and air. The nearest major international airport is in Jaipur (JAI). Major train connections link Jaipur to Delhi, Mumbai, Agra and other Indian cities.

History & Heritage

A short timeline

Founding

Jaipur was founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II. It was India's first fully planned city — laid out according to ancient Hindu architectural treatises (Vastu Shastra).

Architectural identity

In 1876, Maharaja Ram Singh painted the city pink to welcome the Prince of Wales. The color came to symbolize hospitality and remains a defining feature of the old city.

Must-see places

The essentials for first-time visitors

Amber Fort

A hilltop fortress with sprawling courtyards, ornate halls and a panoramic view over Maota Lake.

Hawa Mahal

The 'Palace of Winds' — a honeycomb of windows designed to allow royal women to watch street festivals in privacy.

City Palace

A living palace complex with museums, courtyards and a fusion of Rajput and Mughal architecture.

Culture & food

Traditions, crafts and flavors

Art & Crafts

Jaipur is famous for gemstone cutting, block printing, handloom textiles, lacquerware and blue pottery. Local bazaars like Johari and Bapu Bazaar are ideal for shopping and bargaining.

Local cuisine

Try Dal Baati Churma, Laal Maas (spicy mutton curry), Ghevar (sweet) and kachori. Many heritage cafés and rooftop restaurants offer views of the city's terracotta rooftops.

Practical information

Plan your visit

Best time to visit

October to March — cool mornings & comfortable days. Summers are hot (April–June) and monsoon brings short, heavy showers (July–September).

Getting around

Rickshaws, taxis, and app-based cabs are common. For heritage areas, consider walking between nearby attractions to soak in markets and architecture.
